# Cairn Gate Labs — Internal Arms

All arms operate under Cairn Gate Labs LLC. They are not separate legal entities. Accounting tracks them separately by project code.

---

## Advisory (`ADV`)
**What it is:** Paid workflow reviews, architecture consultations, technical advisory retainers.
**First offer:** Private Workflow Review — map workflows, identify 3 automation opportunities, deliver ROI/action plan.
**Constraint:** No paid work begins without Meta conflicts-tool approval on file.
**Not:** Ongoing managed services, staff augmentation, or anything that looks like employment.

## Systems (`SYS`)
**What it is:** Custom AI system builds — memory architectures, document intake pipelines, research automation, knowledge assistants.
**First offer:** Prototype Sprint — 2–4 week build against a specific workflow problem.
**Not:** Generic app development, frontend work, or anything unrelated to AI workflow infrastructure.

## Tools (`TLS`)
**What it is:** Free and paid diagnostic tools that generate qualified inbound leads.
**First tool:** AI Workflow Opportunity Finder — input workflow description, output top automation opportunities + Cairn Gate CTA.
**Not:** SaaS products with ongoing support burden (until capacity justifies it).

## Briefs (`BRF`)
**What it is:** Paid and free research memos, operator playbooks, field notes. The "Cairn Gate publishes when it has something to say" channel.
**First output:** One flagship operator playbook on a specific workflow type.
**Not:** Industry news, trend reports, or content-marketing filler.

## Studio (`STD`)
**What it is:** Internal product experiments. Micro-SaaS, prototype products, speculative builds.
**Constraint:** Studio experiments are incubated, not launched. Public only when ready to hold up.
**Not:** A venture studio — we are not deploying capital into other people's startups.

## IP (`IP`)
**What it is:** Patent filings, invention logs, trade secret documentation, licensing.
**Process:** Invention memo → prior-art search → employer/IP review → provisional or trade-secret decision → only then publish any non-enabling brief.
**Not:** Public notebooks, enabling technical write-ups, or anything that constitutes a public disclosure before the IP posture is decided.

---

## Sequencing (current phase)

1. **Advisory first** — lowest conflict risk, fastest revenue signal, direct thesis validation
2. **Tools second** — runs in parallel, generates inbound, no client dependency
3. **Systems third** — follows from Advisory learnings
4. **Briefs fourth** — follow from real engagements
5. **IP ongoing** — invention logging starts now, regardless of other phases
6. **Studio last** — only after Advisory and Systems have validated at least one product direction
