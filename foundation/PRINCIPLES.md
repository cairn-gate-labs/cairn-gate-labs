# Cairn Gate Labs — Operating Principles

These are concrete, not aspirational. Each one should be testable in a real decision.

---

**1. Show the work.**
Every recommendation Cairn Gate makes, it can demonstrate. We do not sell theory. If we can't build a working version, we don't pitch it.

**2. Name the failure modes first.**
Before describing what a system does, describe how it fails. A system whose failure modes are unknown is a system we don't understand yet.

**3. The brand speaks; the founder doesn't.**
All public output goes under the Cairn Gate name. The founder's identity is not the credibility layer — the work is. This is a deliberate design choice, not modesty.

**4. Protect before publishing.**
Any technical architecture, method, or framework we develop: decide on IP posture before making it public. There is no un-publishing.

**5. Approval gates are not optional.**
No paid work, advisory role, or outside engagement begins without the required Meta conflicts-tool approval. This is not bureaucracy — it is the legal and professional foundation everything else rests on.

**6. One clean-room policy, always.**
Personal devices, personal accounts, personal time. No Meta code, data, tooling, or infrastructure ever touches Cairn Gate work. Document this consistently, not selectively.

**7. Revenue proves the thesis.**
Consulting and advisory work is not just income — it is research. Real clients with real problems tell us whether the thesis is correct. We treat engagements as feedback loops, not just revenue events.

**8. Accounting before scaling.**
Project codes from day one. Separate business banking. Know whether each arm is profitable before adding complexity to it. The LLC is one entity; the P&L cannot be one bucket.

**9. Spin out only when there's a reason.**
One LLC is sufficient until there is an actual trigger: outside capital, co-founder economics, meaningful liability exposure, or a product that needs a clean cap table. Complexity is a cost.

**10. The work earns the brand.**
Cairn Gate's credibility is built by the quality of its output — tools that work, playbooks that are honest, systems that hold up under real conditions. No marketing shortcut substitutes for this.
